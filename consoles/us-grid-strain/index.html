How to read "strain" honestly. The headline number is each region's demand as a % of its own highest demand over the last 7 days — a pressure proxy, not a reserve margin. A region can sit at 100% of its recent peak and still be fine if it has spare capacity; this dashboard can't see true capacity or reserves (that's the grid operators' real-time data). Treat high numbers as "working hard / worth watching," not "about to fail." The interchange arrows are real, directional public data (region-to-region net power flow), but grid physics is loop-governed (Kirchhoff), so flows distribute across all paths — this is the net result, not a simple upstream→downstream chain.
