Unofficial · descriptive global health atlas, not a forecast. Built on the World Bank's open Indicators API. The latest available year differs by country and indicator, and many smaller countries have data gaps. Not for clinical, individual, or policy decisions.

Global Health AtlasLife expectancy, child & maternal survival, immunization, health systems — for the world, income groups, regions & ~55 of the largest countries

How to read this. Every figure is a World Bank reported value for a specific year — always shown next to the number, because the latest available year varies (often 2023–2024 for survival metrics, sometimes earlier for physicians or maternal mortality). This is descriptive: it shows where things stand and how they have moved since 2000. It does not predict the future, and cross-country comparisons carry differences in measurement and completeness.

Data & method. Source: World Bank Open Data — World Development Indicators, pulled live from the public Indicators API v2 (api.worldbank.org/v2) when reachable, otherwise from an embedded snapshot whose vintage is shown above. Indicators: life expectancy SP.DYN.LE00.IN; under-5 mortality SH.DYN.MORT; maternal mortality SH.STA.MMRT; DTP3 SH.IMM.IDPT; measles SH.IMM.MEAS; health expenditure SH.XPD.CHEX.GD.ZS; physicians SH.MED.PHYS.ZS; TB incidence SH.TBS.INCD. The World Bank in turn compiles these from UN agencies (UN IGME, WHO, UNICEF, UN Population Division) and national statistics.
Honesty. All numbers are measured World Bank reported values — but the latest available year differs by country and indicator, so it is always displayed; many smaller economies have gaps, and missing values are shown as "—" rather than guessed. Aggregates are the Bank's population-weighted estimates. The choice of which ~55 countries and 8 indicators to show is interpretive framing. This tool is descriptive, not predictive; it does not forecast or rank causes. No personal or protected health information is used — only public country-level aggregates.
