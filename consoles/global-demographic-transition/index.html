Global Demographic Transition & AgingThe changing shape of populations — the share aged 65+, old-age dependency, fertility, the young, and life expectancy — for the world, income groups, regions & ~55 of the largest countries

How to read this. Every figure is a World Bank reported value for a specific year — always shown next to the number, because the latest available year varies (most population structure measures run through 2024). This is descriptive: it shows where each population stands in the demographic transition and how its shape has moved since 2000. It does not predict the future. The link from an aging population to a heavier non-communicable-disease and long-term-care burden is offered as interpretive context, not a forecast.

Data & method. Source: World Bank Open Data — World Development Indicators, pulled live from the public Indicators API v2 (api.worldbank.org/v2) when reachable, otherwise from an embedded snapshot whose vintage is shown above. Indicators: population ages 65+ SP.POP.65UP.TO.ZS; old-age dependency ratio SP.POP.DPND.OL; total fertility rate SP.DYN.TFRT.IN; life expectancy at birth SP.DYN.LE00.IN; population ages 0–14 SP.POP.0014.TO.ZS; total dependency ratio SP.POP.DPND. The World Bank in turn compiles these from the UN Population Division (World Population Prospects) and national statistics. The World Bank does not publish a population median age indicator, so it is not shown here.
Honesty. All numbers are measured World Bank reported values — but the latest available year differs by country and indicator, so it is always displayed; many smaller economies have gaps, and missing values are shown as "—" rather than guessed (never 0). Aggregates are the Bank's population-weighted estimates. The choice of which ~55 countries and 6 indicators to show, and the framing of the demographic transition, is interpretive. The statement that aging shifts disease burden toward non-communicable disease and long-term care is interpretive context drawn from the epidemiologic-transition literature — it is not derived from these series and is not a forecast. This tool is descriptive, not predictive; it does not project, rank causes, or recommend actions. No personal or protected health information is used — only public country-level aggregates.
