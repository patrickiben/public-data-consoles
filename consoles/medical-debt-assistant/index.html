Medical Debt AssistantReduce or remove medical debt the lawful way — make them prove it's valid, prove they own it, give you charity care you qualify for, and report it accurately

The core leverage. Hospitals routinely sell unpaid bills to debt buyers for pennies on the dollar. The buyer often can't produce proof that it owns your specific debt, or the original itemized bill. Under federal law you can demand that proof — and if a collector can't validate the debt, it generally must stop collecting and stop reporting it. Combined with charity care and accurate-reporting rules, that is how medical debt is lawfully reduced or removed.

What this is. A guide and letter generator for asserting consumer rights under the FDCPA (debt validation & collector conduct), the FCRA (credit-report disputes), the Affordable Care Act §501(r) (nonprofit-hospital financial assistance), and the No Surprises Act (surprise out-of-network bills). It reflects the well-documented fact that debt buyers frequently lack the documentation to prove ownership of a specific medical debt.
Honesty & limits. not legal advice The goal is to make a collector prove the debt is valid, prove it owns it, bill it correctly, and report it accurately, and to help you access assistance you may qualify for — not to dodge a debt you legitimately owe. Never include false statements in a dispute (that is illegal). Deadlines are strict (e.g. a 30-day validation window) and the law varies by state and changes over time. For anything you're unsure about — especially a lawsuit or a debt you might still owe — talk to free legal aid or a consumer attorney. No information you type is collected or sent anywhere.
