River Flood Propagation

Rivers are a directed network — water flows one way, and an upstream rise leads downstream flooding by hours to days. This walks the main‑stem gauge chain, finds the upstream source driving a flood wave, and gives every community below it a lead time — instead of only reacting to wherever the water is loudest right now.

How this works

About. An unofficial prototype that reorganizes NOAA's own public gauge observations & forecasts into the directed river network. It does not forecast better than NWS — it surfaces the propagation: who's upstream, who's downstream, and how much warning the downstream has. Always follow the National Weather Service / National Water Prediction Service and local officials; call 911 in an emergency.
