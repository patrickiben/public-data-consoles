The warning system went dark
FEWS NET — the USAID-funded famine early-warning system — went offline ~30 Jan 2025 and resumed ~Jun 2025; the GRFC 2026 reported the lowest data availability in a decade. The cuts degraded our ability to even see the famine they helped cause.

The survivors, permanently diminished — education & human capital mostly MODELED

The mortality panels count children who die; this counts the far larger group who survive but are permanently diminished — the toddler stunted before age two, the girl pulled from school when the meal that drew her there vanishes. The slow-burn consequence, and an economic-sovereignty story: lost human capital compounds into lost lifetime earnings and GDP. Read as stakes, not a realized count.

The lasting penalty MODELED · structural
A child born today in a low- or middle-income country is projected to lose ~51% of lifetime earnings to nutrition, learning and skills deficits (World Bank HCI+ 2026); female scores run 20 points below male. Childhood stunting alone cuts individual earnings ~10–26% and can lower national GDP by up to ~7%.
Two anti-double-count rules: this counts survivors with stunting / lost schooling, not the child deaths already in the deaths panel; and it shows the human outcome of school feeding, not the McGovern-Dole dollars already in the funding panel. The out-of-school and aid figures are all-donor projections (the US is one of ~3 donors driving ~80% of the cuts), not USAID alone.
Sources: Catholic Relief Services (school meals, 2025) · UNICEF / UNESCO GEM (out-of-school & aid, Sep 2025) · World Bank Human Capital Index Plus (Feb 2026) · World Bank nutrition research. World Bank HCI is live-wireable (HD.HCI.OVRL); shown here as cited figures.
