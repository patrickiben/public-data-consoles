Unofficial · descriptive global atlas of suicide mortality, not a forecast. Built on the World Bank's open Indicators API. The latest available year differs by country, and many countries have data gaps. Not for clinical, individual, or policy decisions.

Global Mental Health & SuicideSuicide mortality rate, the male/female gap, and the wider mental-health burden — for the world, income groups, regions & ~55 of the largest countries

How to read this. Every suicide figure is a World Bank reported value for a specific year — always shown next to the number, because the latest available year varies (most recent harmonized estimates are 2021). This is descriptive: it shows where things stand and how they have moved since 2000. It does not predict the future. We use the language "died by suicide", never "committed", and treat these as a public-health signal, not a judgement of any person or country.
If you or someone you know is struggling: you are not alone, and help is available. In the US/Canada call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline). Elsewhere, find a local helpline via IASP crisis centres or Befrienders Worldwide. This dashboard is country-level public data and offers no individual support.

◆ The wider mental-health burden — context, not measured here interpretive
Suicide is the most severe and most measurable outcome, but it sits on top of a far larger burden of common mental disorders — most of which never reaches care. The World Bank Indicators API has no depression- or anxiety-prevalence series, so the figures below are cited from WHO and IHME and shown as context, not as values measured in this dashboard.

Roughly 1 in 8 people worldwide live with a mental disorder, with depression and anxiety the most common, and in many countries most people affected receive no treatment (WHO, World Mental Health Report 2022; IHME Global Burden of Disease). These are interpretive context figures cited from those sources — they are not in the World Bank API and are not computed here. They are included so the suicide numbers above are read in proportion: the measured rates are the tip of a much larger, largely untreated burden.
Data & method. Source: World Bank Open Data — World Development Indicators, pulled live from the public Indicators API v2 (api.worldbank.org/v2) when reachable, otherwise from an embedded snapshot whose vintage is shown above. Indicators: suicide mortality rate SH.STA.SUIC.P5; male SH.STA.SUIC.MA.P5; female SH.STA.SUIC.FE.P5 (all per 100,000 population). The World Bank in turn compiles these from the WHO Global Health Estimates. Depression / anxiety context figures are cited from WHO World Mental Health Report (2022) and the IHME Global Burden of Disease, and are not drawn from the World Bank API.
Honesty. Suicide figures are measured World Bank reported values — but the latest available year differs (most are 2021) and is always displayed; many economies have gaps, and missing values are shown as "—" rather than guessed (never 0). Aggregates are the Bank's population-weighted estimates. The depression/anxiety burden is interpretive cited context, not measured here. The choice of which ~55 countries to show, and the framing of the male/female gap, is interpretive. This tool is descriptive, not predictive; it does not forecast and does not rank or judge people or countries. No personal or protected health information is used — only public country-level aggregates. Language: we say "died by suicide", not "committed".
