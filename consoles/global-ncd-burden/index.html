Unofficial · descriptive global NCD atlas, not a forecast. Built on the World Bank's open Indicators API. The latest available year differs by country and indicator, and many smaller countries have data gaps. Not for clinical, individual, or policy decisions.

Global NCD Burden & Risk FactorsThe epidemiological transition — premature mortality from heart disease, cancer, diabetes & chronic lung disease, plus tobacco, alcohol & diabetes, for the world, income groups, regions & ~55 of the largest countries

How to read this. Every figure is a World Bank reported value for a specific year — always shown next to the number, because the latest available year varies (premature-NCD mortality is reported through 2021, smoking through 2022, diabetes through 2024). This is descriptive: it shows where the noncommunicable-disease burden stands today and how it has moved since 2000. It does not predict the future, and cross-country comparisons carry differences in measurement and completeness.

Data & method. Source: World Bank Open Data — World Development Indicators, pulled live from the public Indicators API v2 (api.worldbank.org/v2) when reachable, otherwise from an embedded snapshot whose vintage is shown above. Indicators: premature NCD mortality SH.DYN.NCOM.ZS (probability of dying ages 30–70 from cardiovascular disease, cancer, diabetes or chronic respiratory disease); adult tobacco smoking SH.PRV.SMOK; total alcohol consumption per capita SH.ALC.PCAP.LI; diabetes prevalence SH.STA.DIAB.ZS. The World Bank in turn compiles these from WHO, the IDF Diabetes Atlas and national health surveys. The "~3 in 4 deaths" / ~74% NCD share and the ~41 million annual figure are WHO estimates, shown as interpretive context, not World Bank indicators.
Honesty. All numeric series are measured World Bank reported values — but the latest available year differs by country and indicator, so it is always displayed; many smaller economies have gaps, and missing values are shown as "—" rather than guessed. Aggregates are the Bank's population-weighted estimates. The choice of which ~55 countries and 4 indicators to show, and the framing of the "epidemiological transition", is interpretive. This tool is descriptive, not predictive; it does not forecast or diagnose, and it is not a measure of any individual's risk. No personal or protected health information is used — only public country-level aggregates.
