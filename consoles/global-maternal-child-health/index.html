How to read this. Every figure is a World Bank reported value for a specific year — always shown next to the number, because the latest available year varies (often 2023–2024 for mortality, but typically 2018–2019 for skilled birth attendance, antenatal care and stunting, which come from periodic household surveys). This is descriptive: it shows where things stand and how they have moved since 2000. It does not predict the future, and cross-country comparisons carry differences in survey timing, measurement and completeness. Maternal mortality figures are modeled estimates (UN MMEIG).

Data & method. Source: World Bank Open Data — World Development Indicators, pulled live from the public Indicators API v2 (api.worldbank.org/v2) when reachable, otherwise from an embedded snapshot whose vintage is shown above. Indicators: maternal mortality ratio SH.STA.MMRT; neonatal mortality SH.DYN.NMRT; under-5 mortality SH.DYN.MORT; skilled birth attendance SH.STA.BRTC.ZS; antenatal care 1+ visit SH.STA.ANVC.ZS; adolescent fertility SP.ADO.TFRT; total fertility SP.DYN.TFRT.IN; child stunting SH.STA.STNT.ZS. The World Bank in turn compiles these from UN agencies (UN MMEIG for maternal mortality; UN IGME for child & neonatal mortality; WHO/UNICEF and DHS/MICS surveys for coverage and stunting; UN Population Division for fertility) and national statistics.
Honesty. Mortality, fertility and coverage figures are measured World Bank reported values, with maternal mortality and survey-based coverage shown as modeled estimates (UN MMEIG / household surveys). The latest available year differs by country and indicator, so it is always displayed; many economies have gaps, and missing values are shown as "—" rather than guessed. World Bank aggregates are population-weighted estimates. Child stunting has no World Bank world/income/region aggregate, so the regional figure shown is an unweighted mean computed across this atlas's countries with data — clearly labeled, and not a World Bank aggregate. The choice of which ~55 countries and 8 indicators to show is interpretive framing. This tool is descriptive, not predictive; it does not forecast. No personal or protected health information is used — only public country-level aggregates.
