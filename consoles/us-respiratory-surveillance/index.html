U.S. Respiratory Virus SurveillanceWhat's circulating now — influenza, COVID-19 and RSV across outpatient visits, hospitalizations, and wastewater

How to read this. These are surveillance indicators, each on its own scale: the share of medical visits for a respiratory illness, the hospitalization rate per 100,000 people, and a wastewater viral-activity level. They show relative seasonal intensity and direction — not how many people have a virus. Respiratory viruses are strongly seasonal (winter peaks). This is descriptive — it does not predict the coming season.

▤ State wastewater activity — latest week measured
States ranked by mean wastewater viral-activity level (1 Very Low → 5 Very High) for the selected virus, latest reporting week. Small territories with few sites can swing high or low on thin data.
Each state's value is the mean activity category across its reporting wastewater sites for the latest week — a coarse summary, not a population-weighted prevalence. Coverage varies by state; a few jurisdictions report few sites. Off-season, most states sit at Very Low.
◍ What these indicators mean — and don't interpretive
Data & method. Sources, pulled live from the public CDC Socrata API when reachable, otherwise from an embedded snapshot whose vintage is shown above: NSSP Emergency Department Visits — COVID-19, Flu, RSV (dataset vutn-jzwm; % of ED visits, United States); Rates of Laboratory-Confirmed Respiratory Virus-Associated Hospitalizations — RESP-NET (dataset kvib-3txy; weekly rate per 100k, overall age, observed); Wastewater Viral Activity Level — SARS-CoV-2, Influenza A, RSV (dataset atcp-73re; site activity categories summarized to a mean level).
Honesty. These are surveillance indicators, not case counts — they do not tell you how many people are infected. The three panels use different scales and are not directly comparable. Recent weeks are preliminary and revise as reporting completes (flagged above). All indicators are strongly seasonal. This tool is descriptive, not predictive; it does not forecast a season, advise care, or rank risk. No personal or protected health information is used; all figures are public aggregates.
