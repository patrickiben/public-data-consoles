Typical U.S. price—
Totals = a mid-range procedure price + an estimated round-trip flight from the U.S. + lodging for a typical recovery stay (one traveler). They exclude a companion, pre-op trips, lost wages, complications, travel insurance, and follow-up at home. Savings compare to your entered out-of-pocket if provided, otherwise to the typical U.S. price.
⚠ The risks you're trading against important
If you do consider care abroad: choose facilities with international accreditation (e.g. JCI), get the surgeon's credentials and case volume in writing, plan and budget for complications and follow-up at home, avoid flying too soon after surgery (blood-clot risk), arrange records transfer to your home doctor, and consider medical-travel insurance. Bring your home physician into the decision.
Sources & method. Procedure prices are mid-points of modeled ranges widely reported by medical-travel references (e.g. Patients Beyond Borders, Medical Tourism Association) and vary substantially; U.S. prices are typical cash/list figures and differ greatly by region and insurer. Flight and lodging figures are rough estimates. No live pricing feed exists for international procedures, so everything here is embedded reference data with its assumptions stated.
