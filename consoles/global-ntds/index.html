Neglected Tropical DiseasesThe 20 diseases of poverty that affect over a billion people — the burden, the progress toward elimination, and why they stay "neglected"

What these are. NTDs are a group of ~20 conditions — parasitic, bacterial, viral and venomous — that thrive in poverty, in tropical and subtropical settings with unsafe water, poor sanitation and limited health access. They rarely kill in large numbers all at once, so they draw little attention and little research funding — hence "neglected" — yet they blind, disable, disfigure and trap communities in poverty. NTDs are a leading cause of preventable blindness and disability. They are concentrated in sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia and Latin America, and disproportionately affect children and the rural poor. Many are controlled through inexpensive mass drug administration — among the best value-for-money interventions in global health.

◆ Progress & the 2030 roadmap interpretive
WHO's NTD Road Map 2021–2030 targets a 90% reduction in people needing interventions, 100 countries eliminating at least one NTD, and the eradication of two (dracunculiasis and yaws). Progress is real but uneven and was set back by COVID-19 disruptions to mass-treatment campaigns. Interpretive synthesis of WHO reporting.
◍ Why "neglected" interpretive
Interpretive synthesis. The paradox of NTDs: they affect more than a billion people yet attract a tiny fraction of health research funding, because those affected are poor and the diseases are largely absent from wealthy countries — even though many can be controlled or eliminated very cheaply.
