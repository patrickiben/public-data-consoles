River Flood Network

When a place floods, the water came from somewhere upstream. This builds the upstream tributary tree and asks the question a single gauge can't answer: of all the rivers feeding this spot, which one is the driver — the main stem, or a specific tributary that's in flood while the others are calm?

How this works

About. An unofficial prototype that reorganizes NOAA's own public gauge data onto the USGS river network. It does not forecast better than NWS — it attributes a flood to its upstream source across branches, which a per‑gauge page can't. Always follow the National Weather Service / National Water Prediction Service and local officials; call 911 in an emergency.
