Unofficial · descriptive global nutrition & food-security atlas, not a forecast. Built on the World Bank's open Indicators API. The latest available year differs by country and indicator, and many countries report child-nutrition surveys only every few years. Not for clinical, individual, or policy decisions.

Global Nutrition & Food SecurityUndernourishment, child stunting / wasting / underweight, the double burden of malnutrition, and food insecurity — for the world, income groups, regions & ~55 of the largest countries

How to read this. Every figure is a World Bank reported value for a specific year — always shown next to the number, because the latest available year varies (undernourishment runs through ~2023; child anthropometry comes from periodic surveys and can be several years old). This is descriptive: it shows where nutrition stands and how it has moved since 2000, including recent progress and where it has stalled or risen. It does not predict the future; cross-country comparisons carry differences in survey timing and method.

under-nutrition child overweight
The "double burden" is the coexistence of under-nutrition (here: child stunting, or undernourishment for region/income aggregates) and rising child overweight in the same population. World Bank does not publish world or regional aggregates for child overweight or stunting, so the country view pairs each country's own latest stunting and overweight surveys; the region / income views pair the Bank's aggregate undernourishment with the population-weighted average of member-country overweight surveys shown here (a descriptive summary, not the Bank's official aggregate). Years differ by indicator and are shown.

Data & method. Source: World Bank Open Data — World Development Indicators, pulled live from the public Indicators API v2 (api.worldbank.org/v2) when reachable, otherwise from an embedded snapshot whose vintage is shown above. Indicators: prevalence of undernourishment SN.ITK.DEFC.ZS; child stunting SH.STA.STNT.ZS; child wasting SH.STA.WAST.ZS; severe child wasting SH.SVR.WAST.ZS; under-5 underweight SH.STA.MALN.ZS; child overweight SH.STA.OWGH.ZS; severe food insecurity SN.ITK.SVFI.ZS. The World Bank in turn compiles these from FAO (undernourishment and food insecurity, reported in the annual State of Food Security and Nutrition in the World, SOFI), and the UNICEF/WHO/World Bank Joint Child Malnutrition Estimates (anthropometry).
Honesty. All numbers are measured World Bank reported values — but the latest available year differs by country and indicator, so it is always displayed; child-nutrition surveys are periodic and many recent years are blank, shown as "—" rather than guessed. The "interpretive" people-facing-hunger count multiplies undernourishment by population and is a derived approximation, not a World Bank product; FAO's SOFI report places the figure around 700–800 million in recent years. World Bank does not publish world/regional aggregates for stunting, overweight or food insecurity, so those are shown only where the Bank reports them. Aggregates are the Bank's population-weighted estimates. The choice of which ~55 countries and 7 indicators to show is interpretive framing. This tool is descriptive, not predictive; it does not forecast. No personal or protected health information is used — only public country-level aggregates.
