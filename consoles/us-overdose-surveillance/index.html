⚠ Emerging synthetic-opioid early-warning interpretive
A curated watch list of substances reshaping the unregulated opioid supply. This is qualitative early-warning context — not a live count.
These substances mostly fall under ICD-10 T40.4 (synthetic opioids) or T40.6 (other/unspecified narcotics) and are not separately broken out in the provisional death counts above. Granular identification comes from forensic-lab data (DEA NFLIS) and CDC SUDORS toxicology, which lag roughly a year. Compiled from public early-warning sources (CDC HAN, DEA, EMCDDA EU-EWS, UNODC EWA); reflects status as of the embed date below. Naloxone reverses opioids (including fentanyl and nitazenes) but not sedative adulterants like xylazine or medetomidine.
◍ International context interpretive
The synthetic-opioid mortality crisis is concentrated in North America. There is no browser-accessible live international death-count feed (WHO, UNODC and EMCDDA data are not CORS-open and are largely report-based), so this is curated context, not live surveillance.
Sources are authoritative but reported with their own lags and methods; figures across countries are not directly comparable. Curated as of the embed date below.
Data & method. Primary source: CDC NCHS VSRR — Provisional Drug Overdose Death Counts (dataset xkb8-kh2a), pulled live from the public Socrata API when reachable, otherwise from an embedded snapshot whose vintage is shown above. Values are 12-month-ending provisional counts; predicted = CDC's lag-adjusted model estimate (modeled), reported = counts received to date (measured, provisional and incomplete for recent months). Per-state rates use 2024 Census population estimates.
Honesty. Provisional figures revise upward as data completes — recent months are under-counts. This tool is descriptive, not predictive; it does not forecast, advise, or rank causes. No personal or protected health information is used. Emerging-substance and international panels are interpretive context curated from public reports, not live feeds.
